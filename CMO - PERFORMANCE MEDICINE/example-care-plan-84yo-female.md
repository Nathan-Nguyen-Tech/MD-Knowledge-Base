### Strategies for Poor Appetite

#### 1. Eat Small, Frequent Meals
- **5-6 small meals/snacks per day** instead of 3 large meals
- Easier to get calories without feeling too full

#### 2. Use Calorie-Dense Foods
**Add butter, oil, cheese, nuts to everything** (adds calories without adding volume):
- Butter on toast, vegetables, rice
- Olive oil on salads, pasta
- Cheese on everything
- Whole milk (NOT skim) - full-fat yogurt

**Smoothies** - Liquid calories are easier than solid food:
- Whole milk + protein powder + banana + peanut butter + avocado = 500+ calories

#### 3. Make Eating Comfortable
- If heartburn makes eating uncomfortable ‚Üí you won't eat ‚Üí weight loss
- Take GERD medicine 30-60 min before breakfast (not with food)
- Avoid spicy, acidic foods if they worsen heartburn

#### 4. Consider Social Eating
- **Meals on Wheels** - Delivers hot meal + daily social check-in
- **Senior center meals** - Social eating improves appetite
- **Family brings meals** - Easier than cooking

#### 5. If Nothing Helps
- We can prescribe **appetite stimulant medication** (Megace or Marinol)
- Only if non-medication strategies fail

## üö∂ Movement Plan (Preserve Muscle, Prevent Falls)

### Daily Walking - 10-15 Minutes Per Day

**Why**: Maintains leg strength, balance, circulation, mood

**How**:
- Walk indoors (safer than outdoors if balance concern)
- Use assistive device if needed (cane, walker)
- Can split into 2-3 short walks

### Sit-to-Stand Exercise - 3 Times Per Day

**Why**: **THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT EXERCISE** - You need leg strength to get out of chair, off toilet, out of bed

**How**:
1. Sit in sturdy chair (with armrests if needed)
2. Stand up, then sit back down
3. Repeat 5 times
4. Do this 3 times per day (morning, afternoon, evening) = **15 reps/day**

**As you get stronger**: Try to use arms less, rely on legs more

---

### Balance Exercises - Daily (Prevent Falls)

**‚ö†Ô∏è SAFETY: Always do near counter, wall, or sturdy furniture**

Pick 1-2 that feel safe:

**Tandem Stance**:
- Stand with one foot in front of the other (like walking a tightrope)
- Hold for 10 seconds
- Do near counter for safety

**Heel Raises**:
- Stand at counter
- Raise up on toes, hold 3 seconds, lower
- Repeat 10 times

**Single-Leg Stand** (if feel comfortable):
- Stand on one leg near counter/wall
- Hold for 10 seconds
- Repeat other leg
