# Care Planning Product Guide for Business Development & Medical Director Teams

## Overview

This guide explains the **Performance Medicine Care Planning System** - a comprehensive, AI-enabled approach to creating personalized 12-month care plans that integrate preventive care, performance optimization, and patient goals into one unified prescription.

**Target Audience**: Business Development teams, Medical Directors, practice managers who need to understand and communicate the value of the Performance Medicine care planning approach.

**Purpose**: Explain what the care planning system is, why it matters to patients, and how to position it to attract and retain patients.

## What Is the Performance Medicine Care Planning System?

### The Core Concept

Performance Medicine creates **comprehensive, personalized care plans** that:

1. **Start with the patient's goal** - "Play with grandkids," "Run a marathon," "Travel in retirement," "Manage chronic disease while thriving at work"
2. **Assess 8 health systems comprehensively** - Not just labs, but structure (anatomy), function (daily performance), and risk (future outcomes)
3. **Integrate life stage priorities** - Age-appropriate clinical goals that synergize with personal goals (e.g., cardiovascular disease prevention at age 55 supports goal to travel extensively in retirement)
4. **Include ALL evidence-based preventive care** - USPSTF Grade A/B recommendations mapped to the patient's age, sex, and risk factors (so nothing falls through the cracks)
5. **Deliver a unified prescription** - Medical interventions (medications, procedures) AND lifestyle actions (movement, nutrition, sleep, stress management, environment) in one integrated plan
6. **Break it into manageable quarters** - Detailed 3-month plan (Q1) with evolving 12-month roadmap (Q2-Q4) to avoid overwhelm
7. **Track performance, not just disease** - Capacity (what you can do today), Resilience (how quickly you recover), Flexibility (how well you adapt) alongside traditional risk markers

### The Care Plan Document

The care plan is a **written, patient-facing document** (typically 8-15 pages) that includes:

- **Patient's personal health goal** (center point)
- **Life stage context** (what clinical priorities matter at this age)
- **Health Matrix assessment summary** (8 systems √ó 3 domains, traffic-light visualization)
- **Preventive care checklist** (what screenings/vaccinations are due, current, or not applicable)
- **Unified Q1 care plan** (medical + lifestyle, organized by health system)
- **Q2-Q4 roadmap** (evolving plan based on Q1 progress)
- **Monitoring plan** (metrics we're tracking, follow-up schedule)
- **Educational resources** (guides, apps, support contacts)

The patient **leaves the visit with this document in hand** - a complete roadmap for their health for the next 12 months.

## How the System Works: Overview for BD/MD Teams

### Step 1: Comprehensive Health Assessment (Initial Visit)

**What happens**:
- **Intake**: Patient completes intake forms (demographics, medical history, goals)
- **Visit**: Provider conducts comprehensive assessment
  - Detailed history (8 systems review + lifestyle pillars)
  - Complete physical examination (8 systems)
  - Laboratory testing (comprehensive panels for risk + function)
  - Imaging (as indicated by age, risk factors, symptoms)
  - Functional testing (6-minute walk, sit-to-stand, grip strength, etc.)
  - Patient-reported outcomes (energy, sleep, pain, quality of life)

**Time**: Typically 60-90 minutes for initial comprehensive assessment

**Output**: Complete data set for Health Matrix analysis (8 systems √ó 3 domains = 24 assessment cells)

### Step 2: AI-Enabled Care Plan Generation

**What happens**:
- Provider inputs assessment data into Performance Medicine AI system
- AI processes data through structured logic:
  1. **Life stage assignment** (matches patient age to 1 of 9 life stages)
  2. **Health Matrix analysis** (assigns traffic-light status to each of 24 cells)
  3. **Priority system identification** (identifies top 2-3 systems needing intervention)
  4. **Preventive care mapping** (matches patient to USPSTF recommendations based on age/sex/risk)
  5. **Goal synthesis** (connects patient's personal goal to life stage priorities and priority systems)
  6. **Intervention generation** (creates unified prescription: medical + lifestyle for each priority system)
  7. **Metrics selection** (chooses capacity/resilience/flexibility metrics tied to goal)
  8. **Quarterly structuring** (detailed Q1 plan + evolving Q2-Q4 roadmap)

**Time**: AI generates draft care plan in minutes; provider reviews, refines, and finalizes (10-20 minutes)

**Output**: Complete, personalized 12-month care plan document ready to review with patient

### Step 3: Care Plan Review Visit

**What happens**:
- Provider reviews care plan with patient section by section
- Patient asks questions, discusses concerns
- Provider adjusts plan based on patient preferences, barriers, priorities
- Patient signs care plan (agreement to participate)
- Patient receives final copy (digital + printed)

**Time**: 30-45 minutes

**Output**: Patient leaves with comprehensive care plan document, clear next steps, and confidence

### Step 4: Quarterly Follow-Up & Plan Refinement

**What happens**:
- **Q1 (Month 3)**: Comprehensive reassessment
  - Re-evaluate Health Matrix (repeat labs, functional tests, patient-reported outcomes)
  - Assess adherence to Q1 plan
  - Refine Q2 plan based on progress

- **Q2 (Month 6)**: Comprehensive reassessment
  - Update Health Matrix
  - Adjust medications/interventions based on response
  - Refine Q3 plan

- **Q3 (Month 9)**: Check-in visit (can be telehealth)
  - Track progress toward annual goal
  - Troubleshoot barriers
  - Refine Q4 plan

- **Q4 (Month 12)**: Annual comprehensive reassessment
  - Full Health Matrix re-evaluation
  - Celebrate progress
  - Set next year's goals and create new 12-month plan

**Between visits**: Patient tracks daily/weekly metrics (energy, sleep, adherence) via app or portal; messages care team with questions

## The 8 Health Systems + 3 Domains Framework (Quick Reference)

This is the organizing structure for all assessment and care planning.

### 8 Health Systems

1. **Neurological** - Sleep, attention, mood, cognition, autonomic regulation, pain processing
2. **Cardiovascular** - Blood pressure, heart rate, circulation, exercise tolerance
3. **Respiratory** - Breathing capacity, oxygen saturation, airway health
4. **Metabolic/Digestive** - Glucose regulation, lipid metabolism, gut health, energy production (includes thyroid, insulin)
5. **Filtration/Urinary** - Kidney function, fluid/electrolyte balance, toxin clearance
6. **Musculoskeletal** - Strength, mobility, bone density, pain-free movement
7. **Immune** - Infection resistance, inflammatory control, recovery from illness (includes stress hormones: cortisol, catecholamines)
8. **Reproductive/Hormonal** - Sex hormones, reproductive health, sexual function, reproductive cancer screening

### 3 Clinical Domains

- **Structure (C·∫•u Tr√∫c)** - The anatomy/hardware (Is the organ healthy? Any structural damage?)
- **Function (Ch·ª©c NƒÉng)** - Daily performance (How well does it work right now? Can you do what you need to do?)
- **Risk (Nguy C∆°)** - Future outcomes (What's your risk of disease or decline in the future?)

### Traffic Light Status

Each of the 24 cells (8 systems √ó 3 domains) receives a status:
- üü¢ **Green (Optimal)** - No concerns, maintain current state
- üü° **Yellow (Watch)** - Mild abnormalities or early warning signs, monitor closely
- üü† **Orange (Action Needed)** - Moderate abnormalities or functional limitations, intervene now
- üî¥ **Red (High Priority)** - Severe abnormalities or acute concerns, urgent intervention

**Priority systems** (top 2-3 focus areas) are identified based on number of Orange/Red cells.

## Life Stage Health Goals Framework (Quick Reference)

Performance Medicine recognizes that **clinical priorities change across the lifespan**. The care plan integrates age-appropriate clinical goals that synergize with personal goals.

### 9 Life Stages

**Pediatric**:
1. **Infancy (0-1 year)** - Rapid growth, feeding, bonding, developmental milestones, vaccinations
2. **Early Childhood (2-5 years)** - Motor development, language, social skills, injury prevention
3. **Middle Childhood (6-12 years)** - Skill building, peer relationships, healthy habits, school performance

5. **Early Adulthood (26-39 years)** - Performance optimization, reproductive health, stress management, injury prevention
6. **Middle Adulthood (40-54 years)** - Chronic disease risk reduction, functional capacity preservation, cancer screening

8. **Older Adulthood (65-79 years)** - Functional independence, fall prevention, chronic disease management, cognitive preservation

Each stage has **specific clinical priorities** (e.g., "Aggressive cardiovascular disease prevention" for Stage 7) that the AI automatically pulls into the care plan based on patient age.

## Preventive Care Integration (Quick Reference)

Performance Medicine systematically integrates **all USPSTF Grade A/B recommendations** into care plans based on patient age, sex, and risk factors.

**Key preventive care by age** (abbreviated):

**Ages 18-39**:
- Blood pressure screening (every visit)
- Diabetes screening (if BMI ‚â•25 or risk factors)
- Cervical cancer screening (women, Pap + HPV every 5 years)
- STI screening (if sexually active)
- Depression screening
- Obesity screening + behavioral counseling
- Tobacco/alcohol screening + cessation counseling

**Ages 40-49**:
- All of above, PLUS:
- Lipid screening (every 5 years)
- Breast cancer screening (women, mammography - some guidelines start at 40, USPSTF says 50)

**Ages 50-64**:
- All of above, PLUS:
- Colorectal cancer screening (colonoscopy every 10 years or FIT annually)
- Lung cancer screening (low-dose CT annually if 50-80 with 20 pack-year smoking history)
- Abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA) screening (men 65-75 who ever smoked, one-time ultrasound)

**Ages 65-79**:
- All of above, PLUS:
- Osteoporosis screening (DEXA scan for women 65+, men 70+)
- Fall risk assessment
- Cognitive screening (if concerns)
- Advance care planning discussions

**Ages 80+**:
- Individualized preventive care (discuss risks/benefits of screening vs. quality of life)

**The AI automatically generates a checklist** for each patient showing what's due, current, or not applicable.

## The Unified Prescription: Medical + Lifestyle Integration

Traditional care separates medical interventions (medications, procedures) from lifestyle advice. Performance Medicine integrates both into **one unified prescription**.

### Medical Interventions

**Medications**: Evidence-based pharmacotherapy for priority systems
- Example: Atorvastatin 20mg nightly for elevated LDL (cardiovascular risk reduction)

**Procedures/Screenings**: Diagnostic or preventive procedures
- Example: Colonoscopy for colorectal cancer screening (overdue)

**Supplements**: Evidence-based supplementation for deficiencies or specific indications
- Example: Vitamin D3 2000 IU daily (current level 22 ng/mL, target >30)

### Lifestyle Prescription (5 Pillars)

#### 1. Movement (Musculoskeletal, Cardiovascular, Metabolic systems)
**Specific prescription includes**:
- **Strength training**: Frequency (e.g., 2x/week), duration (30-40 min), exercises (squats, push-ups, rows), intensity (progressive load)
- **Aerobic training**: Frequency (3-4x/week), duration (20-30 min), type (walking, cycling), intensity (Zone 2 = can talk in sentences)
- **Daily movement target**: Steps per day (e.g., 8,000 steps), active minutes, break up sitting every hour

**Purpose**: Build muscle mass, improve insulin sensitivity, increase cardiovascular capacity

#### 2. Nutrition (Metabolic, Cardiovascular, Musculoskeletal, Immune systems)
**Specific prescription includes**:
- **Protein target**: Grams per day (e.g., 100g), distribution across meals (25-30g per meal)
- **Meal timing**: Eating window (e.g., 7am-7pm, 12-hour window), stop eating by [time]
- **Fiber target**: Grams per day (25-30g), food sources (vegetables, whole grains, legumes)
- **Macronutrient targets**: If diabetes, CVD, or performance goal (e.g., carb cycling, higher protein)
- **Hydration**: Liters per day (2-3L), timing (front-load to avoid nighttime urination)

**Purpose**: Support muscle building, metabolic control, cardiovascular health, gut health

#### 3. Sleep & Recovery (Neurological, Immune, Metabolic systems)
**Specific prescription includes**:
- **Sleep window**: Bedtime and wake time (e.g., 10:30pm-6:30am), consistent every day (including weekends)
- **Sleep hygiene**: No screens 60 min before bed, bedroom temperature (65-68¬∞F), blackout curtains, white noise
- **Recovery days**: Frequency (e.g., 1 full rest day per week), active recovery vs. complete rest

**Purpose**: Improve sleep consistency, support HRV recovery, reduce daytime fatigue, enable tissue repair

#### 4. Mental & Emotional Health (Neurological, Immune systems)
**Specific prescription includes**:
- **Stress management**: Daily practice (e.g., 10 min breathwork, meditation), specific technique (box breathing, Headspace app)
- **Mood tracking**: Daily or weekly (0-10 scale), tool (journal, app like Daylio or Bearable)
- **Cognitive tools**: If attention/executive function impaired (e.g., Pomodoro technique, task batching)
- **Therapy**: If indicated (CBT, EMDR, etc.)

**Purpose**: Lower sympathetic tone, improve HRV, support mood, enhance cognitive function

#### 5. Surroundings & Social Context (All systems - enablers)
**Specific prescription includes**:
- **Light exposure**: Morning outdoor light (10-15 min within 1 hour of waking) for circadian health
- **Ergonomics**: Workstation assessment (standing desk, monitor height, chair support) for musculoskeletal health
- **Social connection**: Frequency (e.g., 2 social activities per week), type (walk with friend, family dinner, hobby group)
- **Air quality**: If respiratory or immune concerns (air purifier, reduce allergen exposure)
- **Temperature**: Sleep environment, cold exposure (if applicable)

**Purpose**: Support circadian rhythm, reduce pain, improve adherence through social support, optimize respiratory/immune function

## Quarterly Structure: Avoiding Overwhelm

One of the most important features of Performance Medicine care planning is the **quarterly breakdown**.

### Why Quarterly?

**Problem**: Handing a patient a 12-month plan with 50 action items is overwhelming ‚Üí patient does nothing (analysis paralysis).

**Solution**: Break the plan into **quarterly themes** with 2-3 priorities per quarter.

### Typical Quarterly Progression

**Q1 (Months 1-3): Foundation**
- **Focus**: Establish baseline capacity, address highest-priority risks, build sustainable habits
- **Detail level**: Highly detailed and specific (patient knows exactly what to do)
- **Examples**:
  - Start medications for high-priority risk markers
  - Complete overdue preventive care (colonoscopy, mammography)
  - Establish sleep window, movement routine, nutrition targets
  - Build capacity baseline (functional tests, patient-reported outcomes)

**Q2 (Months 4-6): Build**
- **Focus**: Increase training volume/intensity, optimize interventions, expand capacity
- **Detail level**: Evolving roadmap (refined at Q1 reassessment based on progress)
- **Examples**:
  - Increase strength training frequency (2x ‚Üí 3x per week)
  - Add interval training if cardiovascular capacity improved
  - Adjust medications based on Q1 lab response
  - Address secondary priority systems if primary systems stabilized

**Q3 (Months 7-9): Optimize**
- **Focus**: Fine-tune performance, address marginal gains, challenge the system
- **Detail level**: Evolving roadmap (refined at Q2 reassessment)
- **Examples**:
  - Experiment with metabolic flexibility (longer fasting windows, carb cycling)
  - Add challenging training stimuli (hill sprints, heavy lifts)
  - Taper medications if targets achieved and sustained
  - Focus on resilience and flexibility metrics

**Q4 (Months 10-12): Sustain**
- **Focus**: Maintain gains, prepare for next year's goals, celebrate progress
- **Detail level**: Evolving roadmap (refined at Q3 check-in)
- **Examples**:
  - Maintain training volume, focus on consistency
  - Lock in sustainable habits for long-term adherence
  - Complete annual preventive care
  - Set next year's goals (often more ambitious now that capacity improved)

### Key Principle: Evolving Roadmap

- **Q1 plan is detailed** (patient leaves initial visit knowing exactly what to do for 3 months)
- **Q2-Q4 plans are intentionally vague** (will be refined at each quarterly follow-up based on actual progress)
- **This prevents overwhelm** while still showing the patient there's a long-term vision

**Example language in care plan**:
> **Quarter 2 (Months 4-6): Build (Evolving Roadmap)**
>
> Focus: Increase training volume, optimize medications based on Q1 response
>
> Projected targets:
> - Increase strength training to 3x/week with progressive load
> - Add interval training 1x/week (if cardiovascular capacity improved)
> - Taper atorvastatin if LDL <70 and no side effects
> - Focus on metabolic flexibility (experiment with 14-16 hour fasting windows)
>
> **Note**: This plan will evolve based on your Q1 progress. We'll refine Q2 at your 3-month follow-up.

## Tracking Performance: Capacity, Resilience, Flexibility

Performance Medicine tracks **performance outcomes** alongside traditional risk markers.

### Capacity: What You Can Do Today

**Functional capacity measures**:
- 6-minute walk test (distance in 6 minutes)
- Sit-to-stand test (reps in 30 seconds)
- Grip strength (kg)
- Stairs climbed without breathlessness
- Balance tests (single-leg stand time, tandem walk)

**Patient-reported capacity measures**:
- Energy level (0-10 scale, morning and afternoon)
- Sleep quality (0-10 scale)
- Pain interference (0-10 scale)
- Activity tolerance (can complete desired activities without payback)

**Example**:
> **Baseline**: Energy 4/10 by 2pm, 6-minute walk 380m, sit-to-stand 9 reps
> **Target**: Energy 7/10 through full day, 6-minute walk >400m, sit-to-stand ‚â•12 reps

### Resilience: How Quickly You Recover

**Recovery time measures**:
- Heart rate recovery (HR drop 1 minute after stopping exercise)
- Post-exertion fatigue duration (hours/days to return to baseline energy)
- Sleep latency after stress (can you still fall asleep on a hard day?)
- Illness duration (how quickly you recover from colds)

**Pattern stability measures**:
- Day-to-day energy variability (consistent 7/10 vs. swinging 3-9/10)
- Sleep consistency (same bedtime/wake time across nights)
- Pain pattern predictability (stable baseline vs. frequent flares)

**Example**:
> **Baseline**: Post-run fatigue 2-3 days, HR recovery 15 bpm drop, injury every 6-8 weeks
> **Target**: Post-run fatigue <24 hours, HR recovery ‚â•25 bpm drop, zero injuries in 12 weeks

### Flexibility: How Well You Adapt

**Metabolic flexibility**:
- Fasted state comfort (can skip breakfast without crashing?)
- Post-meal glucose stability (quick return to baseline)

**Training flexibility**:
- Cross-modal performance (can do strength AND endurance without breaking down)
- Recovery from varied stimuli (heat, cold, novel movements)

**Stress flexibility**:
- HRV responsiveness (HRV drops with stress but recovers quickly)
- Mood regulation across life disruptions

**Example**:
> **Baseline**: Must eat within 30 min of waking (crashes if fasted), HRV 40ms (low variability)
> **Target**: Can delay breakfast 2-3 hours comfortably, HRV >60ms with good variability

### Why This Matters

**Traditional medicine**: "Your A1c is 7.2%, blood pressure is 135/85, LDL is 130. We need to get those numbers down."

**Performance Medicine**: "Your A1c, blood pressure, and cholesterol are elevated, which increases your long-term risk. But more importantly, you told me you want to play with your grandkids without getting tired. Right now, your 6-minute walk test shows you're below normal for your age, your energy crashes by 2pm, and it takes you 2-3 days to recover from exertion. Let's build your capacity, resilience, and flexibility so you can do what matters to you - and along the way, we'll improve those risk markers too."

**Patients care more about performance than risk markers** - the care plan speaks their language.

## Common Patient Questions & Answers for BD Teams

### Q: "How is this different from concierge medicine?"

**A**: Concierge medicine typically offers **longer visit times and better access** (same-day appointments, direct cell phone access to doctor), but often uses the **same reactive, disease-focused care model** - just with more time and convenience.

Performance Medicine offers a **fundamentally different clinical model**:
- **Proactive, not reactive** (optimize performance and prevent disease, not wait-and-treat)
- **Performance-focused, not just disease-focused** (capacity, resilience, flexibility are clinical endpoints)
- **Comprehensive care planning** (12-month roadmap, not visit-by-visit care)
- **Lifestyle as medicine** (integrated prescription, not afterthought advice)

You get the **best of both**: the access and time of concierge medicine PLUS a superior clinical model focused on performance and prevention.

### Q: "Do you take insurance?"

**A**: *(This will depend on your specific practice model - adjust accordingly)*

Performance Medicine is typically a **hybrid model**:
- **Comprehensive assessments, care planning, and quarterly follow-ups** are membership/out-of-pocket (not typically covered by insurance because they exceed standard visit times and include extensive care coordination)
- **Labs, imaging, procedures, and medications** are often billable to insurance (we'll maximize your insurance benefits for these)
- **Some practices** participate with insurance for standard visits but charge a separate care planning fee

We provide **superbills** (detailed receipts) that you can submit to your insurance for potential reimbursement if you have out-of-network benefits.

### Q: "How much time does this require from me?"

**A**:
- **Initial comprehensive assessment**: 60-90 minutes
- **Care plan review visit**: 30-45 minutes
- **Quarterly follow-ups**: 30-60 minutes (Q1 and Q2 are longer, Q3 can be brief telehealth)
- **Daily time investment**: Varies by plan, but typically:
  - Movement: 30-60 min/day (includes daily walk + 2-3 structured workouts per week)
  - Nutrition: Meal planning/prep time (varies, but front-loaded - gets easier over time)
  - Sleep: 7-9 hours + 30 min wind-down routine
  - Stress management: 10-15 min/day
  - Tracking: 2-5 min/day (energy, sleep, adherence in app)

**Total**: Less time than most people spend scrolling social media, but vastly more impactful for health and quality of life.

### Q: "What if I can't follow the plan perfectly?"

**A**: **Perfection is not the goal** - progress is. The plan is designed to be **flexible and adaptive**:
- We focus on **2-3 priorities per quarter**, not 50 things at once
- We **adjust based on your real-life constraints** (travel, work schedule, family demands)
- We **celebrate progress**, not punish imperfection
- **Adherence itself is tracked and troubleshot** - if something isn't working, we modify the plan

The goal is **sustainable improvement**, not short-term perfection that leads to burnout.

### Q: "I'm already seeing a cardiologist / endocrinologist / etc. Do I still need them?"

**A**: Performance Medicine is designed to **coordinate with specialists**, not replace them. Here's how it works:

- **For stable chronic conditions** (e.g., well-controlled diabetes, hypertension): Performance Medicine can often **manage these comprehensively** without needing ongoing specialist visits (we'll co-manage with your specialist or transition care if appropriate)

- **For complex or unstable conditions** (e.g., heart failure, advanced kidney disease, difficult-to-control diabetes): You'll **continue seeing your specialist**, and Performance Medicine will **coordinate** - we'll communicate with your specialist, integrate their recommendations into your unified care plan, and ensure lifestyle interventions support medical management

- **For acute issues** (e.g., you need a cardiac catheterization, joint replacement): Performance Medicine will **refer to specialists** and then **reintegrate you** into comprehensive care once acute issue is resolved

**The key difference**: Even if you see specialists, **Performance Medicine is the quarterback** - we coordinate everything, ensure nothing falls through cracks, and keep your care aligned with your personal health goals.

### Q: "What if I just want to optimize performance, not manage disease?"

**A**: Perfect - that's what Performance Medicine was designed for. Many patients come to us **before disease develops**:
- **Athletes** wanting to optimize training, recovery, and performance
- **High performers** (executives, physicians, entrepreneurs) wanting to maintain peak cognitive and physical capacity under high stress
- **Younger patients** wanting to establish healthy habits and prevent disease proactively

Your care plan will focus on:
- **Capacity building** (strength, endurance, cognitive function)
- **Resilience optimization** (faster recovery, stress adaptation)
- **Flexibility enhancement** (metabolic flexibility, training adaptability)
- **Preventive care** (ensure you're up to date on age-appropriate screenings)

No disease diagnosis required - **performance optimization is the goal itself**.
