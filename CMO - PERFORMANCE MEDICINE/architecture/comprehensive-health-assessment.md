# Comprehensive Health Assessment in Performance Medicine

## Overview

Health assessment in Performance Medicine is **multipart and comprehensive**. It uses multiple evaluation tools and methods to determine the status of the 8 health systems across 3 clinical domains (Structure, Function, Risk).

The assessment is not just one examinationâ€”it's the integration of:
- Medical history
- Physical examination
- Vital signs and biometrics
- Laboratory testing
- Imaging studies
- Functional testing
- Patient-reported outcomes

All of these assessment tools work together to paint a complete picture of health status within the Health Matrix framework.

## The Comprehensive Assessment Components

### 1. Medical History

**Purpose**: Understand the patient's health story, risk factors, and context

#### Complete Medical History
- **Chief complaint** - What brings the patient in today?
- **History of present illness (HPI)** - Detailed timeline and characteristics of current concern
- **Past medical history** - All previous diagnoses, chronic conditions, hospitalizations
- **Past surgical history** - All surgeries and procedures
- **Medications** - Current prescriptions, over-the-counter drugs, dosages, adherence
- **Allergies** - Drug allergies, environmental allergies, reactions
- **Family history** - Health conditions in parents, siblings, grandparents (cardiovascular disease, diabetes, cancer, autoimmune conditions, mental health)
- **Social history** - Occupation, living situation, relationships, stress level, tobacco/alcohol/substance use
- **Lifestyle history** - Exercise habits, nutrition patterns, sleep quality, stress management

#### System-Specific History
For each of the 8 health systems, targeted questions:

**Neurological:**
- Sleep duration, quality, snoring, apnea symptoms
- Headaches, dizziness, numbness, tingling
- Mood, anxiety, depression, cognitive changes
- Stress levels and coping mechanisms

**Cardiovascular:**
- Chest pain, palpitations, shortness of breath
- Exercise tolerance changes
- Leg swelling, claudication
- Family history of heart disease, stroke

**Respiratory:**
- Shortness of breath at rest or with exertion
- Cough, wheezing, sputum production
- Asthma, COPD, allergies
- Smoking history (pack-years)

**Metabolic/Digestive:**
- Weight changes, energy levels
- Hunger patterns, food intolerances
- Reflux, bloating, constipation, diarrhea
- Diabetes symptoms (polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia)
- Thyroid symptoms (temperature sensitivity, hair loss, fatigue)

**Filtration/Urinary:**
- Urination frequency, urgency, nocturia
- Changes in urine color or volume
- Kidney stones, infections
- Swelling in extremities

**Musculoskeletal:**
- Joint pain, stiffness, swelling
- Back or neck pain
- Muscle weakness or atrophy
- Functional limitations (stairs, carrying, bending)
- Falls or balance issues

**Immune:**
- Frequency and duration of infections
- Autoimmune conditions
- Chronic inflammation symptoms
- Allergies and sensitivities
- Stress response patterns

**Reproductive/Hormonal:**
- Menstrual cycle regularity, symptoms (women)
- Sexual function, libido
- Menopausal symptoms (women)
- Erectile function (men)
- Prostate symptoms (men)
- Cancer screening history (breast, cervical, prostate, testicular)

### 2. Physical Examination

**Purpose**: Assess structure and function through direct observation and testing

#### General Appearance
- Overall health status
- Body habitus and proportions
- Level of distress
- Grooming and hygiene

#### Vital Signs
- **Blood pressure** (seated, both arms if first visit or discrepancy)
- **Heart rate** (resting, regularity)
- **Respiratory rate** (breaths per minute)
- **Temperature** (if indicated)
- **Oxygen saturation** (SpO2 at rest, with exertion if indicated)

#### Biometric Measurements
- **Height and weight** â†’ BMI calculation
- **Waist circumference** (metabolic risk indicator)
- **Hip circumference** â†’ waist-to-hip ratio
- **Body composition** (if available - DEXA, bioimpedance, skinfold)

#### System-Specific Physical Examination

**Neurological Exam:**
- Mental status (orientation, memory, attention)
- Cranial nerves (vision, eye movement, facial sensation/strength, hearing, swallowing)
- Motor function (strength, tone, bulk, coordination)
- Sensory function (light touch, pain, vibration, proprioception)
- Reflexes (deep tendon reflexes, Babinski)
- Gait and balance (tandem walk, Romberg test)
- Cerebellar function (finger-to-nose, heel-to-shin)

**Cardiovascular Exam:**
- Inspection (jugular venous distension, edema)
- Palpation (point of maximal impulse, peripheral pulses - radial, brachial, femoral, popliteal, dorsalis pedis, posterior tibial)
- Auscultation (heart sounds, murmurs, rubs, gallops)
- Blood pressure (multiple positions if indicated - orthostatic vitals)
- Capillary refill

**Respiratory Exam:**
- Inspection (chest wall symmetry, work of breathing, accessory muscle use)
- Palpation (chest expansion, tactile fremitus)
- Percussion (resonance, dullness)
- Auscultation (breath sounds, wheezes, crackles, rhonchi)

**Metabolic/Digestive Exam:**
- Inspection (abdominal contour, surgical scars, skin changes)
- Auscultation (bowel sounds)
- Palpation (tenderness, masses, organomegaly - liver/spleen edge)
- Percussion (liver span, shifting dullness)
- Visual assessment of body composition and fat distribution

**Filtration/Urinary Exam:**
- Inspection (edema in extremities, periorbital swelling)
- Palpation (costovertebral angle tenderness - kidneys)
- Auscultation (renal artery bruits if hypertension)
- Fluid status assessment (skin turgor, mucous membranes)

**Musculoskeletal Exam:**
- Inspection (alignment, symmetry, muscle bulk, deformities)
- Palpation (tenderness, warmth, swelling, crepitus)
- Range of motion (active and passive - all major joints)
- Strength testing (manual muscle testing, 0-5 scale)
- Special tests (joint-specific provocation tests)
- Spine examination (curvature, mobility, tenderness)
- Gait analysis

**Immune/Integumentary Exam:**
- Skin inspection (rashes, lesions, color, turgor)
- Lymph node palpation (cervical, axillary, inguinal)
- Signs of acute or chronic inflammation
- Nail and hair assessment (nutritional status, systemic disease)

**Reproductive/Hormonal Exam:**
- Breast examination (women - masses, tenderness, nipple discharge)
- Pelvic examination (women - as indicated)
- Testicular examination (men - masses, tenderness)
- Prostate examination (men - size, nodules, as indicated)
- Signs of hormonal imbalance (hirsutism, gynecomastia, androgenic alopecia)
- Tanner staging (adolescents)

### 3. Laboratory Testing

**Purpose**: Assess risk markers, organ function, and metabolic status

#### Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)
- **Glucose** (fasting) - diabetes screening
- **Electrolytes** - sodium, potassium, chloride, bicarbonate
- **Kidney function** - BUN, creatinine, eGFR
- **Liver function** - AST, ALT, alkaline phosphatase, bilirubin, albumin
- **Calcium** - bone health, parathyroid function

#### Lipid Panel (Fasting)
- **Total cholesterol**
- **LDL cholesterol** (calculated or direct)
- **HDL cholesterol**
- **Triglycerides**
- **Advanced lipid markers** (when indicated):
  - ApoB (apolipoprotein B - cardiovascular risk)
  - Lp(a) (lipoprotein(a) - genetic cardiovascular risk)
  - LDL particle number and size

#### Diabetes/Metabolic Markers
- **Hemoglobin A1c** (3-month glucose average)
- **Fasting insulin** (insulin resistance assessment)
- **Oral glucose tolerance test** (if prediabetes suspected)

#### Complete Blood Count (CBC)
- **Red blood cells** - hemoglobin, hematocrit (anemia, oxygenation)
- **White blood cells** - total and differential (infection, immune function)
- **Platelets** - clotting function

#### Inflammatory Markers
- **hsCRP** (high-sensitivity C-reactive protein - cardiovascular and systemic inflammation)
- **ESR** (erythrocyte sedimentation rate - inflammation)

#### Thyroid Hormones (Metabolic System)
- **TSH** (thyroid-stimulating hormone)
- **Free T4, Free T3** (if TSH abnormal or symptoms present)
- **Thyroid antibodies** (if autoimmune thyroid suspected)

#### Sex and Reproductive Hormones (When Indicated)
- **Testosterone** (total and free - men and women)
- **Estradiol, progesterone** (women - cycle-dependent)
- **FSH, LH** (fertility, menopause assessment)
- **DHEA-S** (adrenal androgen)
- **Sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG)** - bioavailable hormone assessment
- **Prolactin** (if menstrual irregularity, galactorrhea)

#### Stress and Adrenal Hormones
- **Cortisol** (AM level, or 4-point salivary if adrenal dysfunction suspected)
- **ACTH** (if adrenal insufficiency suspected)

#### Other Important Markers
- **Vitamin D (25-OH)** - bone health, immune function

#### Kidney-Specific Markers
- **Urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR)** - early kidney damage
- **Urinalysis** - protein, blood, glucose, ketones, casts

#### Liver-Specific Markers
- **GGT** (gamma-glutamyl transferase - liver inflammation)
- **Hepatitis panel** (if risk factors)

#### Immune/Inflammatory Markers
- **Immunoglobulins** (IgG, IgA, IgM - if recurrent infections)
- **Autoimmune panels** (ANA, RF, anti-CCP - if indicated)

#### Nutritional Markers
- **Iron studies** (ferritin, TIBC, transferrin saturation)
- **B12, folate**
- **Magnesium, zinc** (if deficiency suspected)
- **Omega-3 index** (RBC fatty acid profile)

### 4. Imaging Studies

**Purpose**: Assess structural integrity and disease presence

#### Cardiovascular Imaging
- **Electrocardiogram (ECG)** - rhythm, ischemia, chamber enlargement
- **Echocardiogram** - chamber size, wall thickness, valve function, ejection fraction
- **Carotid ultrasound** - plaque burden, stenosis
- **Coronary artery calcium (CAC) score** - atherosclerotic plaque burden
- **Stress testing** (exercise or pharmacologic) - ischemia detection
- **Cardiac MRI or CT angiography** - advanced structural assessment

#### Metabolic/Digestive Imaging
- **Abdominal ultrasound** - liver (fatty liver), gallbladder, spleen, kidneys
- **DEXA scan** - body composition (lean mass, fat mass, visceral fat)
- **CT or MRI abdomen** - detailed organ assessment if indicated
- **Endoscopy or colonoscopy** - GI tract visualization (screening or symptoms)

#### Musculoskeletal Imaging
- **X-rays** - bones, joints (fractures, arthritis, alignment)
- **DEXA scan** - bone mineral density (osteoporosis screening)
- **MRI** - soft tissue (ligaments, tendons, cartilage, discs)
- **Ultrasound** - soft tissue (tendon tears, inflammation)
- **CT scan** - complex fractures, spine

#### Neurological Imaging
- **Brain MRI** - structural abnormalities, white matter changes, atrophy
- **CT head** - acute hemorrhage, stroke, trauma
- **Nerve conduction studies / EMG** - peripheral nerve function

#### Respiratory Imaging
- **Chest X-ray** - lungs, heart size, mediastinum
- **CT chest** - detailed lung parenchyma (nodules, interstitial disease)
- **Pulmonary function tests (PFTs)** - technically functional but often grouped with imaging workup

#### Filtration/Urinary Imaging
- **Kidney ultrasound** - size, structure, masses, stones
- **CT urogram** - kidney stones, obstruction
- **Renal artery Doppler** - renal artery stenosis

#### Reproductive/Hormonal Imaging
- **Mammography** - breast cancer screening (women)
- **Breast ultrasound or MRI** - further evaluation of masses (women)
- **Pelvic ultrasound** - uterus, ovaries, fibroids, cysts (women)
- **Testicular ultrasound** - masses, varicocele (men)
- **Prostate ultrasound (TRUS)** - prostate evaluation (men)
- **DEXA scan** - bone density (hormonal influence on bone health)

### 5. Functional Testing

**Purpose**: Assess performance capacity and system function in action

#### Cardiovascular Functional Tests
- **6-minute walk test** - distance covered, dyspnea scale
- **Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET)** - VO2max, anaerobic threshold
- **Heart rate recovery** - HR drop 1-2 minutes post-exercise
- **Orthostatic vital signs** - BP/HR supine â†’ standing (autonomic function)

#### Metabolic Functional Tests
- **Oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT)** - glucose handling over time
- **Resting metabolic rate (RMR)** - indirect calorimetry
- **Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM)** - 24/7 glucose trends

#### Musculoskeletal Functional Tests
- **Sit-to-stand test** (30 seconds or 60 seconds) - lower extremity strength/endurance
- **Grip strength** (dynamometer) - upper extremity strength
- **Timed up-and-go (TUG)** - mobility and fall risk
- **Single-leg stance** - balance (eyes open, eyes closed)
- **Functional movement screen (FMS)** - movement quality
- **Gait speed** - 4-meter or 10-meter walk
- **Stair climb test** - functional capacity

#### Neurological Functional Tests
- **Cognitive screening** (MoCA, MMSE) - cognitive function
- **Balance tests** (Berg Balance Scale, tandem walk)
- **Reaction time tests**
- **Heart rate variability (HRV)** - autonomic nervous system balance

#### Respiratory Functional Tests
- **Spirometry** - FVC, FEV1, FEV1/FVC ratio (lung volumes and airflow)
- **Peak expiratory flow** - airway obstruction
- **Oxygen saturation on exertion** - desaturation during activity

### 6. Patient-Reported Outcomes (PROs)

**Purpose**: Capture lived experience, symptoms, and functional impact

#### Capacity Measures
- **Energy level** (0-10 scale, daily or weekly)
- **Activity tolerance** (can complete desired activities without excessive fatigue?)
- **Pain interference** (how much does pain limit activity? 0-10)
- **Sleep quality** (0-10 scale, hours slept, feeling rested)

#### Resilience Measures
- **Recovery time after exertion** (hours or days to return to baseline)
- **Illness recovery duration** (how long to recover from colds, infections)
- **Stress recovery** (how quickly do you bounce back from stressful events?)

#### Flexibility Measures
- **Fasting tolerance** (can you skip a meal without crashing?)
- **Travel adaptation** (how quickly do you adjust to time zone changes?)
- **Schedule disruption tolerance** (can you maintain function despite changes?)

#### Mental Health and Quality of Life
- **PHQ-9** (depression screening)
- **GAD-7** (anxiety screening)
- **Stress scales** (Perceived Stress Scale)
- **Quality of life** (SF-36 or similar)
- **Sleep quality** (Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index)

#### Functional Capacity Self-Report
- **Activities of daily living (ADLs)** - bathing, dressing, eating, toileting, transferring
- **Instrumental ADLs (IADLs)** - cooking, cleaning, shopping, managing finances
- **Exercise capacity** - stairs climbed, walking distance, exercise minutes/week

## The Complete Assessment Workflow

### Step 1: Comprehensive Data Collection
Gather data from ALL sources:
- âœ… Full medical history (including family history, surgeries, medications)
- âœ… Vital signs and biometrics
- âœ… Physical examination of all systems
- âœ… Laboratory testing (comprehensive metabolic panel, lipids, A1c, CBC, etc.)
- âœ… Imaging studies (as indicated by history and exam)
- âœ… Functional testing (strength, endurance, balance, cognitive)
- âœ… Patient-reported outcomes (symptoms, function, quality of life)

### Step 2: Map Findings to the Health Matrix
Organize all findings into 8 Systems Ã— 3 Domains (24 cells)

### Step 3: Assign Traffic Light Status
For each system and domain:
- ðŸŸ¢ **Green**: Optimal or on target
- ðŸŸ¡ **Yellow**: Watch zone - monitor
- ðŸŸ  **Orange**: Action needed - moderate priority
- ðŸ”´ **Red**: High priority - immediate intervention

### Step 4: Assess Capacity, Resilience, Flexibility
- **Capacity**: What can they do today? (Functional tests + PROs)
- **Resilience**: How quickly do they recover? (Recovery patterns + HRV)
- **Flexibility**: How well do they adapt? (Metabolic, circadian, stress flexibility)

### Step 5: Generate Problem List and Prioritize
- Reds first (safety, capacity limiters)
- Oranges second (capacity/resilience drains)
- Yellows for monitoring
- Greens to protect and maintain

### Step 6: Create Unified Prescription
- Medical interventions (medications, procedures, referrals)
- Lifestyle interventions (Movement, Nutrition, Sleep, Mental Health, Environment)
- Monitoring plan (what, how, when)

### Step 7: Follow-Up and Reassessment
- Repeat relevant assessments (labs, vitals, functional tests, PROs)
- Update Health Matrix with new data
- Adjust prescription based on response
- Track trends over time

## Summary

**Comprehensive health assessment is multipart and systematic:**

1. **Medical History** - Full story including family, surgeries, medications, lifestyle
2. **Physical Examination** - Vital signs, biometrics, system-by-system exam
3. **Laboratory Testing** - Metabolic panels, lipids, hormones, inflammatory markers
4. **Imaging Studies** - Structural assessment via ultrasound, X-ray, MRI, CT, echo
5. **Functional Testing** - Performance capacity (walk tests, strength, balance)
6. **Patient-Reported Outcomes** - Symptoms, function, quality of life

**All of these tools work together to determine how the 8 health systems are functioning** across Structure, Function, and Risk domains.

The Health Matrix organizes this complexity into a clear, actionable dashboard that guides prioritization and intervention through the unified prescription model.
