# Knowledge Domains Structure

## Overview

This document outlines the comprehensive knowledge domains that comprise the Performance Medicine AI Agent's expertise. Each domain represents a critical component of the integrated Performance Medicine specialty.

---

## Domain 1: Family Medicine

### Scope
Comprehensive, continuous healthcare for individuals and families across all ages, sexes, and organ systems.

### Core Competencies
- Preventive care and health maintenance
- Acute and chronic disease management
- Well visits and screening protocols
- Minor procedures and office-based interventions
- Prenatal and pediatric care (when applicable)
- Geriatric medicine
- Continuity of care and care coordination
- Whole-person, biopsychosocial approach

### Key Knowledge Areas
- USPSTF screening guidelines
- Immunization schedules (CDC/ACIP)
- Common acute illnesses (URI, UTI, gastroenteritis, etc.)
- Chronic disease management (diabetes, hypertension, hyperlipidemia)
- Mental health screening and initial management
- Dermatologic conditions
- Women's and men's health

### Integration with Performance Medicine
Family Medicine provides the foundational primary care knowledge and patient-centered approach that grounds Performance Medicine in comprehensive, longitudinal care.

---

## Domain 2: Internal Medicine

### Scope
Adult medicine with emphasis on complex, multi-system disease diagnosis and management.

### Core Competencies
- Complex medical problem-solving
- Multi-organ system diseases
- Hospital medicine and acute care
- Subspecialty knowledge across all internal medicine fields
- Evidence-based medicine application
- Clinical reasoning and diagnostic excellence

### Key Knowledge Areas
- Cardiovascular medicine
- Pulmonary medicine
- Gastroenterology and hepatology
- Nephrology
- Endocrinology and metabolism
- Hematology
- Oncology (screening and basic management)
- Infectious diseases
- Rheumatology
- Critical care principles

### Integration with Performance Medicine
Internal Medicine provides deep pathophysiology understanding and complex disease management essential for optimizing health in the presence of medical conditions.

---

## Domain 3: Sports Medicine

### Scope
Prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of injuries and illnesses related to exercise and athletics.

### Core Competencies
- Musculoskeletal injury assessment
- Concussion and brain injury management
- Exercise-related medical conditions
- Return-to-play decision-making
- Athletic performance considerations
- Injury prevention strategies
- Rehabilitation oversight

### Key Knowledge Areas
- Orthopedic injuries (sprains, strains, fractures, dislocations)
- Overuse injuries (stress fractures, tendinopathies)
- Concussion protocols (SCAT, return-to-play)
- Exercise-induced conditions (EIB, heat illness, hyponatremia)
- Pre-participation physical examination
- Biomechanical assessment
- Sport-specific injury patterns
- Doping and performance-enhancing substances

### Integration with Performance Medicine
Sports Medicine provides the injury management and athletic performance framework critical for active individuals and performance optimization.

---

## Domain 4: Exercise Physiology and Kinesiology

### Scope
Scientific study of human movement, exercise responses, and physiological adaptations to physical activity.

### Core Competencies
- Exercise prescription for health and performance
- Energy system understanding (aerobic, anaerobic)
- Training adaptations (cardiovascular, muscular, neuromuscular)
- Periodization and programming
- Exercise testing and assessment
- Biomechanics and movement analysis

### Key Knowledge Areas
- Cardiovascular physiology (VO2max, cardiac output, stroke volume)
- Metabolic systems (glycolysis, oxidative phosphorylation, ATP-PC)
- Muscle physiology (fiber types, hypertrophy, strength adaptations)
- Neuromuscular function
- Exercise programming principles (FITT principle, progressive overload)
- Periodization models (linear, undulating, block)
- Movement patterns and functional assessment
- Exercise for disease prevention and management

### Integration with Performance Medicine
Exercise Physiology provides the scientific foundation for exercise prescription and training optimization across all populations.

---

## Domain 5: Functional Medicine

### Scope
Systems biology-based approach addressing root causes of disease through personalized, patient-centered care.

### Core Competencies
- Root cause analysis
- Systems thinking and interconnected physiology
- Personalized, precision medicine approach
- Advanced diagnostic testing interpretation
- Nutrient biochemistry
- Environmental and lifestyle factor assessment

### Key Knowledge Areas
- Core imbalances (hormonal, inflammatory, digestive, detoxification, immune, structural, mental-emotional)
- Gastrointestinal health (microbiome, leaky gut, SIBO)
- Mitochondrial function and cellular energy
- Methylation and genetic polymorphisms (MTHFR, etc.)
- Hormone optimization (thyroid, adrenal, sex hormones)
- Toxin exposure and detoxification pathways
- Food sensitivities and elimination diets
- Nutrient deficiencies and supplementation

### Integration with Performance Medicine
Functional Medicine provides the systems-based, root cause framework for understanding complex health issues and optimizing physiology at a cellular level.

---

## Domain 6: Lifestyle Medicine

### Scope
Evidence-based therapeutic approach using lifestyle interventions as primary modality to prevent, treat, and reverse chronic disease.

### Core Competencies
- Six pillars implementation (nutrition, physical activity, sleep, stress management, social connection, avoidance of risky substances)
- Behavior change strategies
- Motivational interviewing
- Sustainable lifestyle modification
- Population health approaches

### Key Knowledge Areas
- Whole food, plant-predominant nutrition
- Physical activity recommendations (150+ min/week, resistance training)
- Sleep hygiene and optimization (7-9 hours, circadian rhythm)
- Stress management techniques (mindfulness, meditation, breathing)
- Social connection and loneliness intervention
- Tobacco cessation, alcohol moderation
- Lifestyle intervention for specific diseases (diabetes reversal, CAD regression)
- American College of Lifestyle Medicine (ACLM) protocols

### Integration with Performance Medicine
Lifestyle Medicine provides the evidence-based framework for sustainable behavior change and disease prevention through lifestyle interventions.

## Domain 7: Integrative and Complementary Medicine

### Scope
Evidence-based integration of complementary therapies with conventional medicine to treat the whole person.

### Core Competencies
- Evaluation of complementary modalities for evidence and safety
- Mind-body medicine
- Botanical and supplement knowledge
- Manual therapies and acupuncture (referral and collaboration)
- Integration of conventional and complementary approaches

### Key Knowledge Areas
- Mind-body practices (meditation, yoga, tai chi, qigong)
- Herbal medicine and botanical supplements (efficacy, safety, interactions)
- Nutritional supplements (vitamins, minerals, omega-3s, probiotics)
- Acupuncture and traditional Chinese medicine principles
- Manual therapies (chiropractic, osteopathic manipulation, massage)
- Integrative oncology
- Evidence evaluation for CAM therapies
- National Center for Complementary and Integrative Health (NCCIH) resources

### Integration with Performance Medicine
Integrative Medicine broadens the therapeutic toolkit with evidence-based complementary approaches that enhance conventional care.

## Domain 8: Nutrition Medicine

### Scope
Clinical application of nutrition science for disease prevention, treatment, and performance optimization.

### Core Competencies
- Medical nutrition therapy
- Macronutrient and micronutrient physiology
- Dietary pattern recommendations
- Nutritional assessment and intervention
- Supplement evaluation
- Sport nutrition
- Clinical nutrition for disease states

### Key Knowledge Areas

### Integration with Performance Medicine

## Cross-Domain Integration Principles

### Synergistic Application
Knowledge domains are not isolated - they work synergistically:

**Example: Type 2 Diabetes in an Executive**
- Internal Medicine: glycemic control, medication management
- Lifestyle Medicine: dietary intervention, physical activity prescription
- Nutrition Medicine: carbohydrate quality and timing, meal composition
- Exercise Physiology: HIIT and resistance training for insulin sensitivity
- Behavioral Health: behavior change support, stress management
- Executive Health: time-efficient protocols, travel strategies
- Longevity Medicine: cardiovascular risk optimization, healthspan extension

### Evidence Hierarchy
All domains prioritize:
1. High-quality RCTs and systematic reviews
2. Professional society guidelines
3. Expert consensus
4. Mechanistic reasoning (with appropriate caveats)

### Practical Application
Knowledge translates through:
- Clinical protocols
- Individualized plans
- Patient education
- Monitoring and adjustment
