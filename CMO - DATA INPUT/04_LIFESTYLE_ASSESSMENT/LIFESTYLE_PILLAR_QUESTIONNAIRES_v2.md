## Document Overview

This document contains questionnaires for assessing the **5 Lifestyle Pillars**. Each question is written in simple language with evidence-based health recommendations embedded to educate patients while assessing their current habits.

### The 5 Lifestyle Pillars

1. **Nutrition** - What you eat and drink
2. **Movement** - Physical activity and exercise
3. **Sleep & Recovery** - Rest, sleep, and stress management
4. **Mental Health & Emotional Care** - Mood, feelings, and emotional wellbeing
5. **Environment** - Your surroundings, relationships, and community

### How Scoring Works

- **Each Question:** Patients rate themselves from 0 to 10
  - **0** = Not doing this at all / Never
  - **5** = Doing this sometimes / Moderately
  - **10** = Doing this all the time / Completely

## OVERALL LIFESTYLE COMPOSITE SCORE

**LIFE_006 - Lifestyle Composite Score (0-10)**

### How It's Calculated

Average of the 5 pillar scores (equal weighting):
- Nutrition (LIFE_001): 20%
- Movement (LIFE_002): 20%
- Sleep & Recovery (LIFE_003): 20%
- Mental Health (LIFE_004): 20%
- Environment (LIFE_005): 20%

**Formula:**
```
LIFE_006 = (Nutrition + Movement + Recovery + Mental Health + Environment) √∑ 5
```

### Dashboard Integration

1. Multiply LIFE_006 by 10 to get 0-100 points
2. This contributes 30% to your Overall Health Score
3. The other 70% comes from medical test results

## HOW SCORES ARE INTERPRETED

### Individual Pillar Scores

- **8.0-10.0** = Excellent - Keep up the great work!
- **6.0-7.9** = Good - Minor improvements could help
- **4.0-5.9** = Fair - Let's make a plan together
- **2.0-3.9** = Needs Work - This is a priority to address
- **0-1.9** = Critical - We need to talk about this today

### Alert Levels for Clinicians

- **üî¥ Critical Alert**: Any pillar < 2.0, Mental Health < 3.0
- **üü† High Priority**: Any pillar < 4.0, or 2+ point drop over 6 months
- **üü° Moderate**: Any pillar < 6.0

## ACTION PLANNING FRAMEWORK

After completing each assessment, use low scores to create action plans:

### Step 1: Identify Priorities
- Look at which pillar(s) scored lowest
- Within that pillar, find the specific questions with lowest scores

### Step 2: Discuss with Patient
- "I noticed your score was low for [specific question]. Can you tell me more about that?"
- "What makes [healthy behavior] difficult for you?"
- "What do you think would help?"

### Step 3: Collaborative Goal-Setting
- Patient chooses 1-2 specific areas to work on
- Create SMART goals together (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Example: "I will eat vegetables at dinner 4 times this week" (not "I'll eat healthier")

### Step 4: Action Steps
- Break goal into small steps
- Identify resources or support needed
- Schedule follow-up

### Step 5: Track Progress
- Reassess those specific questions at next appropriate interval
- Celebrate improvements
- Adjust plan if needed

   - Spreads burden over time
   - Dynamic (every visit) for high-impact items
   - Stable factors assessed less often

   - Designed to generate clinical discussions
   - Low scores = intervention opportunities
