# Family History Tier 1 Library v1.0

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-25
**Purpose:** Standardized family history conditions with hereditary significance for risk stratification and preventive screening recommendations

---

## Document Overview

This document defines **15 Tier 1 family history conditions** that significantly impact patient risk assessment and clinical decision-making. Each condition includes:

1. **Patient-Friendly Label** - How condition appears on intake form
2. **Genetic/Hereditary Significance** - Strength of familial association
3. **Risk Impact** - Score modifiers applied to patient's health dashboard
4. **Age Thresholds** - Early-onset vs. standard risk stratification
5. **Affected Relative Impact** - Risk varies by relationship (parent, sibling, child)
6. **Clinical Actions Triggered** - Screening recommendations, genetic counseling needs
7. **Smart Expansion Questions** - Brief follow-ups when condition checked

### Clinical Rationale

Family history is one of the strongest predictors of disease risk, often indicating:
- Shared genetic susceptibility
- Shared environmental/lifestyle factors
- Need for earlier or more intensive screening
- Candidacy for genetic testing

**Focus:** This library emphasizes **actionable** family history - conditions where knowing family history changes clinical management.

## FHX-001: Early Heart Attack or Heart Disease

**Patient-Friendly Label:** Heart attack or heart disease at a young age in family

**Genetic/Hereditary Significance:** HIGH
- Familial hypercholesterolemia
- Premature coronary artery disease genes
- Shared cardiovascular risk factors

**Intake Expansion Questions:**

1. **Who in your immediate family had this?** (Check all that apply)
   - Options: â–¡ Father | â–¡ Mother | â–¡ Brother | â–¡ Sister | â–¡ Child | â–¡ Multiple relatives
   - Clinical significance: Multiple relatives = much higher risk

2. **How old were they when diagnosed or when it happened?**
   - Options: Under 40 | 40-50 | 50-55 (men) or 50-65 (women) | After 65
   - Critical threshold: <55 men, <65 women = "premature CAD"

**Risk Impact - Score Modifiers:**

| Scenario | Cardiovascular-Risk Modifier | Metabolic-Risk Modifier |
|----------|----------------------------|------------------------|
| **1 parent, age >55/65** | -4 points | -2 points |
| **1 parent, age <55/65** | -8 points | -4 points |
| **1 sibling, age <55/65** | -10 points | -5 points |
| **Multiple 1st-degree relatives** | -12 points | -6 points |
| **Affected before age 40** | -15 points | -8 points |

**Clinical Actions Triggered:**
- Lipid panel starting age 20 (vs. 35 standard)
- Consider advanced lipid testing (ApoB, Lp(a))
- Calcium score or CTA if intermediate risk
- Statin therapy at lower thresholds
- Genetic counseling if age <40 or multiple relatives

## CLINICAL INTEGRATION NOTES

### Genetic Counseling Referral Criteria

**Immediate referral indicated if:**
- FHX-002: Any sudden cardiac death <50
- FHX-006: Breast cancer <40 or bilateral or male
- FHX-007: Any ovarian cancer
- FHX-010: Multiple pancreatic cancers
- FHX-012: Early-onset dementia (<60)

**Consider referral if:**
- Multiple cancers of same type in family
- Cancer at unusually young age
- Multiple different cancers (suggesting Lynch syndrome, Li-Fraumeni, etc.)
- Patient expressing anxiety/desire for testing

### Family History Update Frequency

- **Annual update** recommended
- **Immediate update** if new diagnosis in family (especially cancer, sudden death)
- Ongoing family history is dynamic - encourage patients to report changes

### Limitations

- Patient knowledge may be incomplete
- "Heart attack" may be conflated with other cardiac events
- Ages may be estimates
- Paternal family history often less well-known
- Adoption/unknown family history needs separate handling
