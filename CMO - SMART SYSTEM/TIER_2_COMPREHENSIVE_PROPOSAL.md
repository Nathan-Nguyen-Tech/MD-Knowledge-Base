# TIER 2 COMPREHENSIVE PROPOSAL - Revised Card Types

## Executive Summary

The current Tier 2 structure has **10 card types** but is missing several critical intervention categories that are essential for comprehensive health management. This proposal expands Tier 2 to **16 card types** organized under 2 Tier 1 categories.

## PROPOSED TIER 2 STRUCTURE (16 Types)

### TIER 1: MEDICAL CARDS (9 Types)

#### 1. üíä **MEDICATIONS** (~300 cards)
**Definition**: Prescription pharmaceuticals ordered by clinician
- All prescription drugs across therapeutic classes
- Requires prescription
- **Does NOT include**: OTC vitamins/supplements (see Supplements type)

**Examples**: Lisinopril, Metformin, Sertraline

---

#### 2. üî¨ **LABS/TESTS** (~90 cards)
**Definition**: Laboratory tests requiring specimen collection
- Blood, urine, stool, other specimens
- Requires provider order
- Includes point-of-care testing

**Examples**: HbA1c, Lipid Panel, Urinalysis

---

#### 3. üè• **IMAGING STUDIES** (~50 cards)
**Definition**: Radiological and imaging diagnostics
- X-ray, CT, MRI, ultrasound, nuclear medicine
- Requires provider order
- May involve contrast or radiation

**Examples**: Chest X-ray, Abdominal CT, Brain MRI

---

#### 4. üè• **PROCEDURES** (~55 cards)
**Definition**: Medical procedures (diagnostic or therapeutic)
- Invasive or semi-invasive
- One-time or periodic
- Requires clinical skill/facility

**Examples**: Colonoscopy, Joint injection, Skin biopsy, Vaccinations

---

#### 5. ü©∫ **MEDICAL DEVICES/EQUIPMENT** (~40 cards) **[NEW]**
**Definition**: Durable medical equipment for ongoing therapeutic use
- CPAP machines, oxygen concentrators
- Prosthetics, orthotics, assistive devices
- Requires prescription/order but patient uses independently
- **Different from monitoring devices** (see Monitoring type)

**Examples**: CPAP for sleep apnea, Home oxygen, Hearing aids, Walker, Wheelchair

**Rationale**: These are prescribed medical interventions but not medications or procedures. They're ongoing therapeutic devices.

---

#### 6. üìä **MONITORING DEVICES/TRACKING** (~35 cards) **[NEW]**
**Definition**: Equipment and tools for home monitoring and self-tracking
- Blood pressure monitors, glucose meters
- Scales, pulse oximeters, thermometers
- Symptom diaries, tracking apps
- May or may not require prescription

**Examples**: Home BP monitor, Glucometer, Daily weight tracking, Symptom diary

**Rationale**: Critical for chronic disease management. Different from medical devices (therapeutic) and different from labs (not sent to facility). Patient self-monitors at home.

---

#### 7. üíä **SUPPLEMENTS/OTC** (~50 cards) **[NEW]**
**Definition**: Over-the-counter vitamins, minerals, and supplements
- Vitamins, minerals, herbal supplements
- Evidence-based recommendations (not all supplements included)
- **No prescription required** but clinician-recommended

**Examples**: Vitamin D 2000 IU, Calcium + Vitamin D, Omega-3 fatty acids, Probiotics

**Rationale**: Very common interventions. Different from medications (OTC, lower evidence grade typically). Need separate tracking for adherence and safety.

---

#### 8. üß† **BEHAVIORAL HEALTH SERVICES** (~30 cards) **[NEW]**
**Definition**: Mental health and counseling interventions
- Psychotherapy (CBT, DBT, ACT, etc.)
- Counseling (individual, couples, family)
- Support groups
- Requires referral/prescription

**Examples**: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for anxiety, Support group for grief, Couples counseling

**Rationale**: Mental health is critical. Currently buried in "Referrals" but deserves distinct category given prevalence and importance. Different from Mind-Body practices (which are self-directed).

---

#### 9. üë®‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è **SPECIALIST REFERRALS** (~30 cards)
**Definition**: Consultations with medical specialists
- All medical specialties
- Urgent vs routine
- Diagnostic vs therapeutic consultations

**Examples**: Cardiology consult, Endocrinology consult, Physical therapy referral

**Note**: Behavioral health referrals moved to separate type (above)

### TIER 1: BEHAVIORAL CARDS (7 Types)

#### 10. ü•ó **NUTRITION** (~105 cards)
**Definition**: Dietary interventions and eating behaviors
- Dietary patterns, food focus, eating behaviors
- Patient-initiated with clinical guidance
- Evidence-based nutrition science

**Examples**: Mediterranean diet, DASH diet, Increase vegetables

---

#### 11. üèÉ **MOVEMENT/EXERCISE** (~80 cards)
**Definition**: Physical activity and exercise prescriptions
- Aerobic, strength, flexibility, balance
- Structured exercise programs
- Patient-initiated with guidance

**Examples**: Walk 30 min 5x/week, Strength training 3x/week

---

#### 12. üò¥ **SLEEP/STRESS/RECOVERY** (~60 cards)
**Definition**: Recovery-focused behaviors
- Sleep hygiene, stress management, breathing exercises
- Patient-directed practices
- Foundation for health

**Examples**: Consistent sleep schedule, 4-7-8 breathing, Progressive muscle relaxation

---

#### 13. üßò **MIND-BODY PRACTICES** (~45 cards)
**Definition**: Integrative practices combining mental and physical elements
- Meditation, yoga, tai chi, qigong
- Self-directed wellness practices
- Evidence-based for multiple conditions

**Examples**: Mindfulness meditation, Hatha yoga, Tai chi

---

#### 14. üåç **ENVIRONMENTAL/SOCIAL HEALTH** (~60 cards) **[NEW]**
**Definition**: Social determinants of health and environmental modifications
- **Social**: Community engagement, relationships, social support
- **Environmental**: Air quality, housing, neighborhood safety
- **Occupational**: Workplace modifications, ergonomics
- Patient-initiated or requires community resources

**Subcategories**:
- Social Connection & Community
- Housing & Living Environment
- Air Quality & Environmental Toxins
- Occupational Health & Ergonomics
- Neighborhood & Built Environment
- Financial Health & Resource Access

**Examples**:
- Join community group/club
- Improve home air quality (air purifier, reduce allergens)
- Ergonomic workspace setup
- Spend time in nature/green spaces weekly
- Address housing mold/safety issues
- Social connection: call friend/family 3x/week

**Rationale**: Social determinants account for 80% of health outcomes. These interventions are evidence-based and actionable but currently have no home in the system.

---

#### 15. üö≠ **SUBSTANCE USE/HARM REDUCTION** (~40 cards) **[NEW]**
**Definition**: Interventions for substance use, addiction, and harm reduction
- Smoking/vaping cessation
- Alcohol reduction or abstinence
- Substance use support
- Harm reduction strategies

**Examples**:
- Smoking cessation program (pharmacotherapy + counseling)
- Reduce alcohol to <7 drinks/week
- Nicotine replacement therapy
- Attend AA/SMART Recovery meetings

**Rationale**: Extremely prevalent (smoking, alcohol use). High impact on multiple health systems. Currently scattered across medications (varenicline), behavioral health (counseling), and mind-body (stress management). Deserves distinct category for tracking and targeting.

---

#### 16. üìö **MICRO-LEARNING** (~100 cards)
**Definition**: Brief educational content (5-minute lessons)
- Foundational concepts before action cards
- Used within decks for progressive learning
- Knowledge checks included

**Examples**: Understanding BP numbers, What is insulin resistance, Macronutrients explained

## RATIONALE FOR EACH NEW TYPE

### Medical Devices/Equipment (NEW)
**Why separate from Procedures?**
- Procedures are one-time or periodic events
- Devices are ongoing therapeutic interventions
- Different adherence patterns (daily use vs periodic visits)
- Different barriers (equipment maintenance, supplies)

**Examples**: CPAP (sleep apnea), oxygen (COPD), hearing aids (hearing loss), wheelchair (mobility)

### Monitoring Devices/Tracking (NEW)
**Why separate from Labs?**
- Labs are sent to facility; monitoring is patient self-directed at home
- Real-time feedback vs periodic results
- Different skill set (patient must learn to use device)
- Critical for chronic disease management (diabetes, HTN, CHF)

**Examples**: Home BP monitor, glucometer, daily weights, symptom diary

### Supplements/OTC (NEW)
**Why separate from Medications?**
- No prescription required
- Different evidence base (often lower quality)
- Different insurance coverage (usually patient pays)
- Different safety profile (fewer drug interactions)
- Very commonly recommended by clinicians

**Examples**: Vitamin D, calcium, omega-3s, probiotics, fiber supplements

### Behavioral Health Services (NEW)
**Why separate from Referrals?**
- Mental health is critical and prevalent (~1 in 5 adults)
- Ongoing services (not one-time consult like typical referral)
- Different from self-directed mind-body practices
- Needs distinct tracking for sessions, adherence, outcomes

**Examples**: CBT for anxiety, DBT for borderline personality, Support groups, Family therapy

### Environmental/Social Health (NEW)
**Why essential?**
- **Social determinants drive 80% of health outcomes**
- Air quality affects respiratory and cardiovascular health
- Social connection reduces mortality (equal to smoking cessation!)
- Housing quality impacts multiple conditions (asthma, injuries, mental health)
- Occupational health addresses back pain, stress, repetitive injuries
- Evidence-based interventions but currently no home in system

**Examples**:
- Social: Join walking group, volunteer, strengthen relationships
- Environmental: Air purifier, reduce home allergens, test for radon
- Occupational: Ergonomic desk setup, take movement breaks
- Community: Access green spaces, improve neighborhood walkability

### Substance Use/Harm Reduction (NEW)
**Why separate?**
- Extremely prevalent:
  - Smoking: 12% of adults
  - Alcohol use disorder: 5% of adults
  - Other substances: 3-4% of adults
- High impact across ALL health systems
- Currently scattered across multiple types (medications, referrals, behavioral)
- Deserves focused attention for screening, intervention, tracking

**Examples**: Smoking cessation (varenicline + counseling), alcohol reduction, harm reduction strategies

## TIER 2 ORGANIZATIONAL LOGIC

### Medical Cards (9 types) - "What Clinicians Order"
Characteristics:
- Require prescription, order, or referral (with exception of OTC supplements)
- Typically involve healthcare system interaction
- Insurance considerations
- Safety monitoring often required

Types: Medications, Labs, Imaging, Procedures, Medical Devices, Monitoring Devices, Supplements/OTC, Behavioral Health Services, Specialist Referrals

### Behavioral Cards (7 types) - "What Patients Initiate"
Characteristics:
- Patient can start independently (though clinician may recommend)
- Emphasize behavior change and habit formation
- Usually minimal to no cost
- Social and environmental support often needed

Types: Nutrition, Movement, Sleep/Stress/Recovery, Mind-Body, Environmental/Social Health, Substance Use/Harm Reduction, Micro-Learning

## PERFORMANCE MEDICINE FRAMEWORK ALIGNMENT

### How New Types Map to 8 Health Systems

**Medical Devices/Equipment**:
- Respiratory (CPAP, oxygen)
- Musculoskeletal (walkers, wheelchairs, orthotics)
- Neurological (hearing aids)

**Monitoring/Tracking**:
- Cardiovascular (BP monitors)
- Metabolic (glucose monitors)
- Filtration/Urinary (fluid tracking for CHF)

**Supplements/OTC**:
- Musculoskeletal (calcium, vitamin D for bone health)
- Immune (vitamin C, zinc, probiotics)
- Metabolic (fiber supplements)

**Behavioral Health Services**:
- Neurological (mental health is neurological domain)
- Cross-system (mental health affects all systems)

**Environmental/Social Health**:
- Respiratory (air quality)
- Cardiovascular (social connection reduces CVD risk)
- Neurological (social connection, occupational stress)
- All systems (social determinants affect everything)

**Substance Use/Harm Reduction**:
- Respiratory (smoking)
- Cardiovascular (smoking, alcohol)
- Metabolic (alcohol)
- Neurological (addiction, mental health comorbidity)

## QUESTIONS FOR CONSIDERATION

1. Should **Supplements/OTC** be Medical or Behavioral?
   - **Current placement**: Medical (clinician-recommended, evidence-based)
   - **Alternative**: Behavioral (patient-initiated, OTC)
   - **Recommendation**: Keep in Medical (evidence-based, often monitored)

2. Should **Monitoring/Tracking** be Medical or Behavioral?
   - **Current placement**: Medical (devices often prescribed, disease management)
   - **Alternative**: Behavioral (patient self-directed)
   - **Recommendation**: Keep in Medical (critical for clinical decision-making)

3. Should we further split **Environmental/Social Health**?
   - Could separate into: Environmental Health (air, housing) vs Social Health (relationships, community)
   - **Recommendation**: Keep combined initially; use Tier 4 to separate subcategories

4. Any other card types missing?
   - Sexual health?
   - Financial health/resource navigation?
   - Spiritual/religious practices?
   - **Recommendation**: Sexual health ‚Üí Behavioral Health Services; Financial ‚Üí Environmental/Social; Spiritual ‚Üí Mind-Body
