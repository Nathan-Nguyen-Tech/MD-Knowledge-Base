## Card Naming Convention

**Format**: `REF_[TYPE]_[NUMBER]`

**Type Abbreviations**:
- `MED` = Medical Specialist
- `BH` = Behavioral Health
- `REHAB` = Rehabilitation Services
- `ALLIED` = Allied Health

---

## Notes

1. **Referral content**: Include common reasons, what to send, expected wait times
2. **Insurance requirements**: Note if prior authorization typically required
3. **Urgent criteria**: Define what constitutes urgent vs routine referral
4. **Communication**: Include what information to send with referral (records, labs, imaging)
5. **Follow-up**: Include when to follow up if no appointment scheduled

---

## Clinical Pearls for Card Development

1. **Specialist Referral Cards**: Include common indications, what workup to complete before referral
2. **Psychiatry Cards**: Include safety assessment, current medications, what to do while waiting
3. **PT Cards**: Include specific goals, any precautions/restrictions, weight-bearing status
4. **Dietitian Cards**: Include diagnosis, specific diet goals, relevant labs
5. **Cardiac Rehab Cards**: Include qualifying diagnoses, functional status, goals

---

## Referral Letter Components (Template Reference)

### Essential Elements:
1. **Reason for referral** - Specific question or concern
2. **Relevant history** - HPI, PMH pertinent to referral
3. **Medications** - Current medication list
4. **Allergies** - Drug allergies
5. **Recent workup** - Labs, imaging, studies already done
6. **Urgency** - Routine, urgent, emergent
7. **Contact information** - How to reach referring provider

### Specialty-Specific:
- **Cardiology**: Recent ECG, echo, stress test results
- **GI**: H. pylori status, prior endoscopy reports
- **Nephrology**: eGFR trend, urine studies
- **Oncology**: Pathology reports, staging imaging
