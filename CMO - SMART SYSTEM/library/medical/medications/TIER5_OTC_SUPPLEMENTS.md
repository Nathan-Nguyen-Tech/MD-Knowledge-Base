## Card Naming Convention

**Format**: `OTC_[SUBCLASS]_[NUMBER]`

**Subclass Abbreviations**:
- `VIT` = Vitamins
- `MIN` = Minerals
- `HERB` = Herbal/Natural (CV/Metabolic)
- `GI` = GI/Digestive Supplements
- `JOINT` = Joint/Musculoskeletal
- `SLEEP` = Sleep/Stress
- `PAIN` = OTC Analgesics
- `GIMED` = OTC GI Medications
- `ALLERGY` = OTC Allergy/Cold

## Notes

1. **Evidence grades**: Many supplements have limited evidence (Grade C-D); include this in cards
2. **Drug interactions**: Fish oil (bleeding), St. John's Wort (many interactions), Red Yeast Rice (statin interactions)
3. **Quality concerns**: Supplements not FDA-regulated; recommend USP/NSF verified products
4. **Max doses**: OTC analgesics have strict daily maximums - emphasize in cards
5. **Not included**: Weight loss supplements, performance enhancers, "detox" products

---

## Clinical Pearls for Card Development

1. **Vitamin D Cards**: Include testing recommendations, toxicity risk at very high doses, calcium co-administration
2. **Iron Cards**: Include GI side effects, vitamin C co-administration for absorption, avoid with certain meds
3. **Fish Oil Cards**: Include bleeding risk, fishy aftertaste management, triglyceride reduction evidence
4. **Probiotic Cards**: Include specific strains for specific conditions, storage requirements
5. **OTC PPI Cards**: Include 14-day courses, not for long-term use without MD supervision
6. **Acetaminophen Cards**: Emphasize hepatotoxicity risk, alcohol interaction, max daily dose

---

## Quality Verification Symbols (Include in Cards)

Recommend looking for these third-party verification marks:
- **USP Verified** - United States Pharmacopeia
- **NSF Certified** - NSF International
- **ConsumerLab Approved** - Independent testing
- **Informed Sport** - For athletes (drug-free)
