## Card Naming Convention

**Format**: `MED_PAIN_[SUBCLASS]_[NUMBER]`

**Subclass Abbreviations**:
- `NSAID` = Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs
- `TOPNSAID` = Topical NSAIDs
- `APAP` = Acetaminophen
- `NEURO` = Neuropathic Pain (Anticonvulsants)
- `ANTIDEP` = Antidepressants for Pain
- `TOPNEURO` = Topical Neuropathic Agents
- `OPSA` = Short-Acting Opioids
- `OPLA` = Long-Acting Opioids
- `MR` = Muscle Relaxants

## Notes

1. **Opioid prescribing**: All opioid cards require CDC guideline compliance, PDMP checks, informed consent
2. **Combination products**: Listed as combination when clinically prescribed that way (e.g., hydrocodone/APAP)
3. **Cross-reference**: Gabapentin/pregabalin also in Neuro/Psych as anti-seizure; duloxetine also as antidepressant
4. **OTC NSAIDs/APAP**: Lower doses covered under OTC/Supplements category
5. **Controlled substances**: Opioids (Schedule II-IV), carisoprodol (Schedule IV) require DEA compliance

---

## Clinical Pearls for Card Development

1. **NSAID Cards**: Include GI protection (PPI), CV risk, renal monitoring, avoid in CKD/CHF
2. **Opioid Cards**: Include MME calculations, naloxone co-prescribing, constipation prophylaxis, tapering guidance
3. **Gabapentin/Pregabalin Cards**: Include titration schedule, renal dosing, dizziness/sedation warnings
4. **Muscle Relaxant Cards**: Include sedation risk, avoid in elderly (Beers criteria), short-term use
5. **Methadone Cards**: Include QT prolongation monitoring, complex pharmacokinetics, respiratory depression risk
6. **Fentanyl Patch Cards**: Include opioid-tolerant only requirement, heat exposure warning, disposal instructions
