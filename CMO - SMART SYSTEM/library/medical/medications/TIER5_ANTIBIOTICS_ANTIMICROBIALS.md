## Notes

1. **Antibiotic stewardship**: All cards should emphasize appropriate indication, duration, and de-escalation
2. **Drug interactions**: Fluoroquinolones + cations, macrolides + QT drugs, azoles + CYP interactions
3. **Allergy cross-reactivity**: Penicillin-cephalosporin (~1-2%), sulfa drugs
4. **Not included**: Most IV-only hospital antibiotics, HIV antiretrovirals (separate category)
5. **Combination regimens**: H. pylori, TB, and multi-drug regimens covered in separate protocol cards

---

## Clinical Pearls for Card Development

1. **Fluoroquinolone Cards**: Include FDA black box (tendon, nerve, aorta), avoid in children, QT prolongation
2. **Azithromycin Cards**: QT prolongation risk, drug interactions
3. **TMP/SMX Cards**: Sulfa allergy, hyperkalemia, Stevens-Johnson risk
4. **Clindamycin Cards**: C. diff risk
5. **Fluconazole Cards**: Drug interactions (statins, warfarin), QT prolongation
6. **Antiviral Cards**: Timing critical (within 48-72h of symptoms), renal dosing
