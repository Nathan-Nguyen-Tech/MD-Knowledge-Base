# SMART CARD LIBRARY - TIER 2 OUTLINE

## Overview
Tier 2 divides the 2 top-level categories into 10 specific card types, each representing a distinct intervention modality.

**Total Card Types**: 10
- Medical Cards: 5 types
- Behavioral Cards: 5 types

---

## MEDICAL CARDS (5 Types)

### 1. MEDICATION CARDS üíä
**Definition**: Prescription or OTC medications ordered by a clinician for treatment or prevention.

**Scope**:
- Prescription medications (all drug classes)
- OTC medications when clinically indicated
- Biologics and specialty medications
- Topical, oral, injectable, inhaled routes

**Key Features**:
- Dosing (with titratable ranges in single card)
- Administration instructions
- Monitoring requirements (labs, vitals)
- Drug interactions and contraindications
- Safety warnings by health system

**Estimated Cards**: ~300-350
**Tier 3 Categories**: 12
- Cardiovascular Medications
- Diabetes Medications
- Respiratory Medications
- Gastrointestinal Medications
- Neurological Medications
- Psychiatric Medications
- Antibiotics/Antimicrobials
- Pain Management Medications
- Endocrine Medications
- Rheumatologic/Immunologic Medications
- Oncology Medications
- Other Medications

**Example Cards**:
- Lisinopril (ACE inhibitor for hypertension)
- Metformin (first-line for Type 2 diabetes)
- Albuterol inhaler (SABA for asthma)
- Omeprazole (PPI for GERD)
- Levothyroxine (thyroid hormone replacement)

---

### 2. LAB/TEST CARDS üî¨
**Definition**: Laboratory tests and diagnostic studies requiring specimen collection (blood, urine, stool, etc.).

**Scope**:
- Blood tests (chemistry, hematology, immunology)
- Urine tests
- Stool tests
- Other specimen-based diagnostics

**Key Features**:
- Specimen type and collection method
- Preparation requirements (fasting, medication holds)
- Reference ranges and interpretation
- Frequency of monitoring
- When to escalate based on results

**Estimated Cards**: ~80-100
**Tier 3 Categories**: 6
- Blood Tests (majority)
- Urine Tests
- Stool Tests
- Other Specimen Tests
- Point-of-Care Tests
- Cardiac/Metabolic Markers

**Example Cards**:
- Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP)
- Hemoglobin A1c
- Lipid Panel
- TSH (thyroid function)
- Urinalysis

---

### 3. IMAGING CARDS üè•
**Definition**: Radiological and imaging studies requiring specialized equipment.

**Scope**:
- X-rays
- CT scans
- MRI
- Ultrasound
- Echocardiography
- Nuclear medicine studies

**Key Features**:
- Indication for study
- Preparation requirements
- Contrast media considerations
- Radiation exposure (if applicable)
- Results timeline
- Follow-up based on findings

**Estimated Cards**: ~40-50
**Tier 3 Categories**: 1 comprehensive file
- All Imaging Modalities (grouped by body system/indication)

**Example Cards**:
- Chest X-ray
- Abdominal CT scan
- Brain MRI
- Carotid ultrasound
- Echocardiogram (transthoracic)

---

### 4. PROCEDURE CARDS üè•
**Definition**: Medical procedures requiring clinical skill, often invasive or semi-invasive.

**Scope**:
- Diagnostic procedures (endoscopy, colonoscopy)
- Therapeutic procedures (joint injections, minor surgeries)
- Screening procedures
- Preventive procedures (vaccinations)

**Key Features**:
- Purpose/indication
- Preparation (bowel prep, fasting, medication holds)
- Sedation requirements
- Recovery time
- Post-procedure instructions
- Complications to watch for

**Estimated Cards**: ~50-60
**Tier 3 Categories**: 1 comprehensive file
- All Procedures (grouped by body system)

**Example Cards**:
- Colonoscopy
- Upper endoscopy (EGD)
- Joint injection (corticosteroid)
- Skin biopsy
- Influenza vaccination

## BEHAVIORAL CARDS (5 Types)

### 6. NUTRITION CARDS ü•ó
**Definition**: Dietary interventions, eating patterns, and nutrition-focused actions.

**Scope**:
- Dietary patterns (Mediterranean, DASH, low-carb)
- Macronutrient focus (protein, carbs, fats)
- Specific food focus (increase vegetables, reduce sodium)
- Eating behaviors (mindful eating, intermittent fasting)
- Meal planning and prep

**Key Features**:
- Specific dietary change
- Portion sizes and servings
- Food examples (include/avoid/limit)
- Meal timing
- Prep tips and recipes
- Budget considerations

**Estimated Cards**: ~100-120
**Tier 3 Categories**: 5
- Dietary Patterns
- Macronutrient Focus
- Specific Food Focus
- Eating Behaviors
- Meal Planning & Timing

**Example Cards**:
- Follow Mediterranean diet
- DASH diet for blood pressure
- Increase protein intake to 1g/lb
- Eat 5 servings vegetables daily
- Practice intermittent fasting (16:8)

---

### 7. MOVEMENT CARDS üèÉ
**Definition**: Physical activity and exercise prescriptions.

**Scope**:
- Aerobic exercise (walking, running, cycling, swimming)
- Strength training
- Flexibility and stretching
- Balance training
- Sport-specific activities
- Physical therapy exercises

**Key Features**:
- Exercise type and modality
- Intensity (RPE, heart rate, METs)
- Duration and frequency
- Progression tiers
- Equipment needed
- Safety modifications
- Technique cues

**Estimated Cards**: ~80-100
**Tier 3 Categories**: 5
- Aerobic Exercise
- Strength Training
- Flexibility & Stretching
- Balance Training
- Specialized Movement

**Example Cards**:
- Walk 30 minutes, 5x/week at moderate pace
- Bodyweight strength training 3x/week
- Daily stretching routine (10 minutes)
- Tai Chi for balance
- Cycling 20 minutes, 4x/week

---

### 8. RECOVERY CARDS üò¥
**Definition**: Actions focused on rest, recuperation, and stress management.

**Scope**:
- Sleep hygiene and optimization
- Stress management techniques
- Breathing exercises
- Progressive muscle relaxation
- Rest and recovery protocols

**Key Features**:
- Specific recovery action
- Duration and frequency
- Step-by-step technique
- Tools/resources needed
- Expected benefits
- When to practice

**Estimated Cards**: ~60-80
**Tier 3 Categories**: 3
- Sleep Actions
- Stress Management
- Breathing & Relaxation

**Example Cards**:
- Maintain consistent sleep schedule
- Practice 4-7-8 breathing for 5 minutes
- Progressive muscle relaxation (15 minutes)
- Digital sunset (no screens 1 hour before bed)
- Power nap (20 minutes)

---

### 9. MIND-BODY PRACTICE CARDS üßò
**Definition**: Integrative practices combining mental focus and physical movement.

**Scope**:
- Meditation and mindfulness
- Yoga
- Tai Chi
- Qigong
- Guided imagery
- Biofeedback

**Key Features**:
- Practice type and style
- Duration and frequency
- Progression (beginner to advanced)
- Mental and physical benefits
- Resources (apps, videos, classes)
- Modifications for limitations

**Estimated Cards**: ~40-50
**Tier 3 Categories**: 1 comprehensive file
- All Mind-Body Practices

**Example Cards**:
- Mindfulness meditation (10 minutes daily)
- Hatha yoga (30 minutes, 3x/week)
- Tai Chi practice (20 minutes, 4x/week)
- Body scan meditation
- Loving-kindness meditation

---

### 10. MICRO-LEARNING CARDS üìö
**Definition**: Brief (5-minute) educational content that provides foundational knowledge before action cards.

**Scope**:
- Cardiovascular concepts (BP, cholesterol)
- Metabolic concepts (blood sugar, insulin)
- Nutrition science (macros, glycemic index)
- Exercise physiology (heart rate zones, RPE)
- Medication mechanisms (how drugs work)

**Key Features**:
- 5-minute time limit
- Concept explanation
- Visual aids (diagrams, videos)
- Knowledge check (3 questions)
- Links to related action cards
- Unlocks subsequent cards in deck

**Estimated Cards**: ~80-100
**Tier 3 Categories**: 4
- Cardiovascular Concepts
- Metabolic Concepts
- Nutrition Science
- Exercise & Movement Concepts

**Example Cards**:
- Understanding Your Blood Pressure Numbers
- What is Insulin Resistance?
- Macronutrients Explained
- Heart Rate Zones for Exercise
- How ACE Inhibitors Work

**Important Note**: Micro-learning cards are typically used WITHIN decks, not prescribed standalone. They provide brief foundational knowledge before action cards.
