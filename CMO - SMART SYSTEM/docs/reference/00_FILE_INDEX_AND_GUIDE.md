## üéØ KEY ARCHITECTURAL CHANGES

### What Changed and Why

**1. Eliminated "Educational Cards" Category**
- **Why**: Education is a feature of every card, not a separate action
- **Now**: Educational content embedded in WHY/HOW sections of all cards
- **Benefit**: No passive learning; every card is actionable

**2. Created SMART Deck Library**
- **Why**: Replace traditional patient education materials (PDFs, printouts)
- **Now**: Condition-based decks with progressive, actionable cards
- **Benefit**: Patients learn by doing, not reading walls of text

**3. Introduced Micro-Learning Cards**
- **Why**: Some concepts need brief intro before action
- **Now**: Optional 5-minute learning cards within decks
- **Benefit**: Foundational knowledge without overwhelming

**4. Simplified Tier 1 Structure**
- **Why**: Medical vs Behavioral is clearest organizational principle
- **Now**: 2 categories instead of 3
- **Benefit**: Easier navigation, clearer mental model

## üéØ REMEMBER: Core Principles (Updated)

No matter which file you're reading, always keep these in mind:

1. **One Action Per Card** - Keep cards focused
2. **Patient Goals First** - Link to intrinsic motivation
3. **Evidence-Based** - Grade all cards (A/B/C)
4. **6th-8th Grade Language** - Patient-facing content must be simple
5. **Safety by Health Systems** - Organize warnings clearly
6. **Dual Confidence Scoring** - AI + Patient scores
7. **Community Wisdom** - Balance evidence with lived experience
8. **Version Control** - Track changes over time
9. **Accessibility Always** - Design for all literacy and tech levels
10. **Co-Navigation** - Patients and clinicians working together
11. **Education Through Action** - No passive learning **(NEW)**
