# COMMUNITY FEEDBACK & REVIEW SYSTEM

## Philosophy: Wisdom of the Crowd

**Core Belief**: Academic research provides the foundation, but real-world patient experience reveals implementation realities. Both are essential.

Balance evidence-based medicine with lived patient experience to create cards that are both clinically sound and practically achievable.

## PATIENT REVIEW PROCESS

### Step 1: Eligibility Requirements

**Minimum Requirements to Leave Review**:
- Enrolled in card for **at least 14 days**
- Attempted to complete the action at least once
- Verified patient identity (not spam/bots)

**Why 14 Days?**
- Ensures reviews based on actual experience, not initial impressions
- Allows time to encounter real barriers and benefits
- Filters out "quit on day 1" reviews

### Step 3: Review Moderation

**All reviews enter moderation queue before publication.**

#### Moderation Criteria

**Approved If**:
- ‚úÖ Appropriate language (no profanity, hate speech)
- ‚úÖ Relevant to the specific card
- ‚úÖ Based on personal experience
- ‚úÖ Constructive (even if negative)

**Rejected If**:
- ‚ùå Contains personal medical information (PHI)
- ‚ùå Spam or promotional content
- ‚ùå Offensive or discriminatory language
- ‚ùå Dangerous misinformation (e.g., "stopped taking heart meds and felt great")
- ‚ùå Not relevant to the card
- ‚ùå Duplicate submission

#### Moderation Team
- Clinical staff (RN, MD, PharmD)
- Trained moderators with clinical oversight
- AI pre-screening for obvious violations
- Human final decision on borderline cases

#### Moderation Response Time
- Target: Within 48 hours of submission
- Patient notified when review is published or rejected

**Rejection Notification**:
```
Thank you for your review. Unfortunately, we cannot 
publish it because [reason]. 

We encourage you to submit a revised review that 
[specific guidance].
```

**Patients can flag published reviews for**:

**When a Review is Flagged**:

2. Human moderator reviews within 24 hours
3. If violation confirmed ‚Üí Review removed

**Abuse Prevention**:

- Users who repeatedly violate guidelines lose review privileges

## PREVENTING REVIEW MANIPULATION

### Safeguards

**1. Verified Patients Only**
- Must be enrolled in card via healthcare provider
- Cannot review without attempting the action
- Account tied to medical record (privacy-protected)

**2. Rate Limiting**
- One review per card per patient
- Can edit/update existing review
- Cannot delete and resubmit to game system

**3. AI Spam Detection**
- Flags suspiciously similar reviews
- Detects copy-paste patterns
- Identifies bot-like behavior

**4. Audit Trail**
- All reviews logged with timestamps
- Moderator decisions documented
- Pattern analysis for gaming attempts

**5. No External Incentives**
- No rewards for positive reviews
- No penalties for negative reviews
- Honest feedback encouraged

## PRIVACY & CONSENT

### Patient Privacy Protection

**Review Anonymization**:
- Display first name + age only ("Maria, 52")
- No last names, addresses, contact info
- No specific medical details beyond card topic
- Geographic location hidden

**Consent Process**:
```
Before submitting review:

‚òê I consent to sharing my anonymous feedback with 
  other patients and healthcare providers.
  
‚òê I understand my review will include my first name 
  and age, but no other identifying information.
  
‚òê I will not include personal medical details beyond 
  my experience with this card.

[Submit Review]
```

**Opt-Out Anytime**:
- Patients can delete their reviews at any time
- Deletion is permanent and immediate
- Does not affect their care

## LEVERAGING COMMUNITY WISDOM

### Best Practices

**For Patients**:
1. Read community reviews before starting a card
2. Look for reviews from people like you
3. Note common barriers and prepare for them
4. Contribute your experience to help others

**For Clinicians**:
1. Use community data to set expectations
2. Pre-emptively address common barriers
3. Choose cards with high adherence for overwhelmed patients
4. Share patient success stories from reviews

**For System Designers**:
1. Continuously improve cards based on feedback
2. Create support resources for common barriers
3. Develop new cards based on patient requests
4. Celebrate and highlight patient successes
