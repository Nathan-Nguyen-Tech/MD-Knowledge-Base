### Why Educational Cards Were Eliminated

**Problem Identified**:
- Education is not a standalone action
- No such thing as "do education" - always "learn about [something]"
- That [something] is already covered by specific card types
- Creates redundancy and confusion

**Solution Implemented**:
- Education is now **embedded within every card**
- WHY sections explain mechanism and benefits
- HOW sections teach proper technique
- Micro-learning cards provide brief intro within decks
- No need for separate "educational" category

## TAG SYSTEM FOR FLEXIBLE FILTERING

Rather than rigid hierarchical organization for cross-cutting concepts, use **TAGS** for flexible filtering. One card can have multiple tags.

### Tag Categories

#### 1. Clinical Intent Tags
- `#PreventiveCare` - Screening, prevention, risk reduction
- `#AcuteCare` - Immediate, urgent conditions
- `#ChronicManagement` - Ongoing disease management
- `#Palliative` - Symptom management, comfort care
- `#Diagnostic` - Testing, diagnosis
- `#Screening` - Asymptomatic screening
- `#Monitoring` - Ongoing surveillance

#### 2. Guidelines/Evidence Tags
Use USA and globally accepted sources only:
- `#USPSTF_GradeA` - US Preventive Services Task Force Grade A
- `#USPSTF_GradeB`
- `#USPSTF_GradeC`
- `#ACC_AHA` - American College of Cardiology/American Heart Association
- `#ADA` - American Diabetes Association
- `#NICE` - UK National Institute for Health and Care Excellence
- `#Cochrane` - Cochrane systematic reviews
- `#KDIGO` - Kidney Disease: Improving Global Outcomes
- `#ATS_ERS` - American Thoracic Society/European Respiratory Society
- `#ACOG` - American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists
- `#AAP` - American Academy of Pediatrics

#### 3. Care Setting Tags
- `#Inpatient` - Hospital admission required
- `#Outpatient` - Clinic or office visit
- `#Home` - Can be done at home
- `#Telehealth` - Virtual/remote care
- `#ICU` - Intensive care unit
- `#ED` - Emergency department
- `#NursingHome` - Long-term care facility

#### 4. Urgency Tags
- `#Emergent` - Immediate, life-threatening
- `#Urgent` - Same-day or within 24 hours
- `#Routine` - Scheduled, non-urgent

## Tag Innovation Features

### 1. Smart Tag Suggestions by AI
When generating or editing a card, AI automatically suggests relevant tags based on:
- Card content analysis
- Clinical context
- Patient population
- Evidence sources cited

### 2. Tag Hierarchies
Parent tags include child tags for filtering:
- `#Cardiology` includes: `#Hypertension`, `#CHF`, `#Arrhythmia`
- `#PreventiveCare` includes: `#Screening`, `#Vaccination`, `#Counseling`

### 3. Required vs Optional Tags
**Required** (minimum 1 from each):
- Clinical Intent
- Care Setting
- Organ System

**Optional but Recommended**:
- Guidelines
- Life Stage
- Condition
- Special Population
- Card Function **(NEW)**

### 4. Version-Controlled Tags
Tags evolve with guideline updates:
- `#ACC_AHA_2017` â†’ Updated to `#ACC_AHA_2024`

- `#RenalDosing` â†’ "Adjusted for Kidney Function"
- `#Geriatric` â†’ "For Older Adults (65+)"

## Tag Management Best Practices

### For Card Creators
1. **Minimum 3 tags** (preferably 5-7)
2. **Cover all required categories** (Intent, Setting, System)
3. **Include guideline tags** when evidence available
4. **Add community tags** based on patient feedback
5. **Update tags** when guidelines change
6. **Tag micro-learning cards** with #MicroLearning **(NEW)**

### For Clinicians
1. **Use tags to filter library** quickly
2. **Combine multiple tags** for precise searches
3. **Save common filter combinations** as presets
4. **Review tag suggestions** from AI before publishing
5. **Filter by #MicroLearning** to find educational intros **(NEW)**

### For Patients
1. **Filter by "Easy" or "Affordable"** to find manageable cards
2. **Use "WorkedForMe" tag** to see what others succeeded with
3. **Avoid "Challenging" tags** if overwhelmed
4. **Check community ratings** alongside tags
